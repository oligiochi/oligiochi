## Introduction

I'm a Computer Engineering graduate student focused on how large systems are built and operated: distributed coordination, backend services, container runtimes, and the networking underneath them. I spend most of my time on Linux, writing Rust, Java, Kotlin, and Python, and I care about software that is measurable, reproducible, and honest about its failure modes. I have an academic background in machine learning, but systems work is where I invest my energy.

## Current Focus

- **Decentralized V2X cooperative smart parking** — my main research project: a fully decentralized vehicle-to-everything system where vehicles cooperate to discover and allocate parking without a central coordinator.
- **Cloud-native and container experiments** — building and breaking things with Docker, Podman, and Kubernetes to understand isolation, orchestration, and the trade-offs between them.
- **Networking and Linux internals** — protocol-level experiments, traffic analysis, and small tools that poke at the kernel's networking stack.
- **Personal open-source projects** — utilities and libraries I publish as they become useful to more people than just me.

## Research Interests

- **Distributed systems** — decentralized coordination, consensus, and cooperative protocols; my current work applies these ideas to V2X scenarios where no central authority can be assumed.
- **Networking** — transport and application-layer protocol behavior, measurement, and the practical gap between protocol specifications and real deployments.
- **Cloud computing** — container isolation and orchestration, and what "cloud native" actually costs in observability and operational complexity.
- **Machine learning** — an academic grounding I draw on when a systems problem genuinely calls for learned components, rather than as an end in itself.
- **Operating systems** — Linux internals, particularly the interfaces containers are built on: namespaces, cgroups, and the networking stack.
